  Strategy Reframe It as Specialization, NOT Repetition

  The officer's concern Why another diploma You already have one.

  Your answer must prove These are two completely different fields that combine to create a unique skill set.

  ---
  How to Write It in the LOJ

  ▎ I hold a Diploma in Applied Arts from India, which gave me foundational knowledge in traditional art forms — drawing, painting, color theory, and print
   design. However, this program did NOT cover any digital or interactive media skills.

  ▎ The Interactive Media Design program at Seneca College is an entirely different discipline focused on digital skills UIUX design, motion graphics,
  video editing, 3D modeling, web design, and interactive storytelling using industry tools like Figma, Adobe After Effects, and Premiere Pro.

  ▎ My academic path is Traditional Arts (foundation) → Interactive Digital Media (specialization). This is vertical progression — I am building ON TOP of
  my existing skills, not repeating them. The combination of traditional art fundamentals + modern digital media tools makes me uniquely competitive in
  India's growing digital design industry.

  ▎ I chose a diploma over a degree because
  ▎ - (a) Seneca's 2-year diploma is industry-focused with hands-on projects and portfolio-building, which is exactly what Indian employers value
  ▎ - (b) A 4-year degree would mean longer time abroad and higher costs, which contradicts my intention to return quickly
  ▎ - (c) This specific program's curriculum directly matches the job roles I am targeting (UX Designer, Motion Graphics Designer) — a degree would include
  unnecessary general courses

  What NOT to Say

  - I want to upgrade my skills — too vague, every applicant says this
  - Canadian education is better than Indian — offensive and untrue, officer will reject this
  - I couldn't find a degree program — sounds like diploma was a backup plan
  - Diploma is cheaper — sounds like you're cutting corners

  Final Line to Add

  ▎ I respectfully submit that my academic path demonstrates clear progression traditional art fundamentals (India) → digital interactive media
  specialization (Canada). This is not a repetition of my education but a deliberate and logical next step that Indian institutions do not currently offer
  at the same practical, industry-aligned level as Seneca College.
